About me

Nice to meet you! My name is Simone Stradiotto, born July/15 /1999 in Italy, where I currently live. The first three adjectives that come to my mind in order to describe myself are curious, stubborn and constant. That said, however, the best thing to say in order to describe me is that I love to read and learn.

I spent countless hours just reading about all kind of things: history, nature, space, technology and science. Usually it's just my curiosity to drive me, but of course there have been times where I needed some specific information for me or for my family. In that occasions I understand that my curiosity, in the course of many years, passively helped me to learn how to learn. All of this thanks to the fact that we live in the greatest time in the entire history to learn new things.

During my adventures on the Internet, I came across a famous quote from the author and public speaker Donald Miller: “In the age of information, ignorance is a choice.” I deeply agree with this statement.

With the web, we are capable to access the greatest library of all times, a literally infinite amount of information, way more than any human could learn in a lifetime. Well, I'm not going to waste this possibility.

My aspirations

Nature

I believe that now it can no longer be denied: as humans, we have greatly damaged our biosphere. Unfortunately, until recently, many people did not see climate changes as a serious and immediate problem. That being said, it gives me hope to see how in recent years, many companies have undergone a process of awareness, trying to reduce their environmental impact. Much of this has been possible thanks to the digital revolution.

Technology

I've always been fascinated by the infinite potential of technology; having no biological restrictions to respect, in the world of technology the only limit is people's imagination. Thanks to it, our species in the last hundred years has been able to make unthinkable cultural advances, and there are no hints of an arrest.

Biodiversity

I grew up and live in a country house. I remember when I was a child, I liked to go out in our garden on summer evenings and observe the fireflies. For several years now the fireflies are no more. It's a very personal example, but it was when I noticed this little thing that I realized how easy it is to pass over or even not notice some ecosystem losses. In this area too, digital can help raise public awareness of this kind of facts.

Innovation

I admire inventors very much. And more than that, I admire the inherent ability in every human being to shape an idea and solve problems. I have enjoyed puzzles and riddles ever since I was a child, and I still enjoy solving the various problems that arise in software development. Obviously it's an area where you never finish learning, but that's just fine for me.

My skills

Hard Skills

Each time I write an HTML file, I try to make it as semantic as possible. I keep attenction on the indentantion and I try do make the file as readable as possible. When I get the occasions, I enjoy experimetn with the really new tags. Of course, if I know that the targets for that site are people who could have older pc and browser, I try to give them too a good experience with the site.

I absolutely LOVE CSS. Sometimes I like to go see some CSS codepen, and I'm always amazed at what can be achieved with only that technology. So, in my sites, I always try to make something cool exploiting the potential of the modern CSS. I also like to use the BEM metodology for classes, which really helps with the DRY mentality.

If the app I'm working on is big enough, I really like to use SASS, expetially with SCSS syntax. It helps not only to speed up the process, but also to keep the code really organized. I'm particulary a fan of the 7-in-1 architecture.

I'm also pretty confident using JavaScript. Everything that cannot be achived with CSS, can probably be achived with JS, and I believe that this can really make or break a web app. Even if I can understand well the old code, I'm more at ease with post ES2015 syntax.

The Poppy War

Written by R. F. Kuang

“The Poppy War” is an historical fantasy published in 2018. It's a fantasy repurposing of the second sino-japanese war (1937–1945), and it also deals with various aspects of Mongolian and Chinese shamanism. I must warn you, however, that it is a book that deals with war in a very raw way, and I do not recommend it if you are easily impressionable.
